Rapé

Sacred Tobacco Medicine

What is Rapé?

Rapé (pronounced "ha-peh" or "rapay") is a sacred shamanic snuff made from powdered Amazonian tobacco and other medicinal plants, ashes, and tree barks. It has been used for thousands of years by indigenous tribes of the Amazon for healing, prayer, and spiritual connection.

This powerful medicine is blown into the nostrils through a ceremonial pipe called a "Tepi" (administered by another person) or "Kuripe" (self-administered), creating an immediate grounding and centering effect.

Each tribe has its own unique blend, often containing specific plants chosen for their healing properties and spiritual significance.

What to Expect in Ceremony

Preparation

Before receiving Rapé, set a clear intention for what you wish to release or receive. The facilitator will prepare the sacred medicine and create a ceremonial space.

During Ceremony

Rapé is administered into both nostrils, one at a time. Upon receiving the medicine:

  • Immediate intense sensation in the sinuses
  • Possible tearing and purging through the nose
  • Instant grounding and presence
  • Mental clarity and stillness
  • Opening of energy channels
  • Deep connection with the present moment

The intense phase lasts only a few minutes, followed by a profound sense of calm, clarity, and connection. Participants are encouraged to sit quietly and integrate the experience.

After Ceremony

Following Rapé, many people experience enhanced mental clarity, emotional balance, and a deep sense of grounding that can last for hours or days.

Rapé is ideal for those seeking quick grounding, mental clarity, and spiritual connection. It's particularly beneficial for:
