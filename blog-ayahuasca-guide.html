Ayahuasca is a powerful plant medicine brew that has been used for thousands of years by indigenous peoples of the Amazon rainforest for healing, spiritual growth, and connection with the divine. In recent years, it has gained worldwide attention for its profound therapeutic benefits in treating depression, anxiety, PTSD, addiction, and facilitating deep personal transformation.

What is Ayahuasca Made From?

Ayahuasca is a sacred brew made from two primary plants found in the Amazon:

  • Banisteriopsis caapi vine - Contains MAO inhibitors that allow DMT to become orally active
  • Psychotria viridis (chacruna) leaves - Contains DMT (dimethyltryptamine), the primary visionary compound

The preparation process is sacred and ceremonial, often taking 8-12 hours of cooking the plants together. Indigenous shamans have perfected this recipe over millennia, and at Sacred Rebirth, we honor these traditional methods.

How Does Ayahuasca Work?

Ayahuasca works on multiple levels - physically, emotionally, psychologically, and spiritually:

The Science

The DMT in ayahuasca activates serotonin receptors in the brain, particularly the 5-HT2A receptor, which is associated with consciousness, perception, and mood regulation. Recent research shows ayahuasca promotes neuroplasticity - the brain's ability to form new neural connections - which may explain its effectiveness in treating depression and trauma.

The Spiritual Dimension

Beyond neurochemistry, ayahuasca is known as "La Medicina" (The Medicine) because it facilitates deep healing on spiritual and energetic levels. Many people report:

  • Profound spiritual experiences and connection with the divine
  • Release of emotional trauma stored in the body
  • Insights into life patterns and purpose
  • Feelings of unconditional love and interconnectedness
  • Visions that provide guidance and clarity

What to Expect in an Ayahuasca Ceremony

At Sacred Rebirth in Valle de Bravo, Mexico, our ceremonies follow traditional protocols in a safe, sacred space:

Ceremony Structure (4-6 hours)

  1. Opening (30 min) - Setting intentions, prayers, and sacred space preparation
  2. Drinking the Medicine (15 min) - Each participant receives their dose
  3. Journey (3-4 hours) - Effects begin 30-60 minutes after drinking
    • Visions (eyes closed or open)

  4. Integration (1 hour) - Gentle return, sharing circle (optional), rest

The Purge: An Essential Part of Healing

Purging (vomiting) during ayahuasca ceremonies is considered a vital part of the healing process. It's not just physical detoxification - indigenous traditions teach that purging releases stuck emotions, trauma, and negative energy. Most participants feel significantly lighter and clearer after purging.

Is Ayahuasca Safe?

When taken in a proper ceremonial setting with experienced facilitators and medical screening, ayahuasca has an excellent safety record. However, it's not for everyone:

Contraindications (Do NOT take ayahuasca if you have):

  • Heart conditions or high blood pressure
  • Taking SSRIs, MAOIs, or certain medications
  • Schizophrenia or psychosis
  • Pregnancy
  • Severe liver or kidney disease

At Sacred Rebirth, we require a thorough health screening to ensure ayahuasca is safe and appropriate for you.

How to Prepare for Your First Ayahuasca Ceremony

  • Alcohol and recreational drugs

Eat:

  • Rice, quinoa, oats

  • Drink plenty of water
